### You're Competing on Multiple Fronts

When a client asks you to find talent, you're not just racing the clock‚Äîyou're competing with:

- **Other recruiting agencies** vying for the same placement fee

**The Problem**:
- ‚ùå **Speed alone isn't enough** - Sending unqualified candidates damages your reputation

- Your candidate pipeline reputation suffers

## Best Practices for Agencies

### 1. Set Quality Standards

**Define Your Bar**:
- Minimum AI score to submit (e.g., 70%+)
- Required rubric criteria scores
- Must-pass questions

**Why It Matters**: Consistency builds client trust. They know your submissions meet a verified standard.

### 2. Use the Two-Layer Approach

**Layer 1 (Your Phone Screen)**: Assess soft skills, motivation, culture fit
**Layer 2 (AceUnlock AI)**: Assess hard skills, technical depth, role competency

**Don't Skip Layer 1**: AI handles technical depth, but you still need to assess fit and motivation.

### 3. Review Transcripts, Not Just Scores

**The Score**: Tells you if they passed your bar
**The Transcript**: Shows you HOW they think and communicate

**Recruiter Tip**: Highlight key transcript excerpts when submitting to clients. Shows depth of your vetting.

### 4. Create Client-Specific Rubrics

**Generic Rubric**: "Technical Skills, Communication, Problem Solving"
**Client-Specific**: "React Experience, API Design, Team Leadership, Startup Experience"

**Result**: Better candidate-client matches, higher placement rates

### 5. Use It as Your Differentiator

**Your Pitch to Clients**:
*"Unlike other agencies that only do phone screens, we conduct AI-powered technical interviews on every candidate. You only see candidates who have already proven their technical abilities. Here's the evaluation report..."*

**Result**: Win more client contracts, command higher fees

### 6. Track ROI Meticulously

**Key Metrics**:
- Candidates screened per placement (should decrease)
- Client interview success rate (should increase)
- Days from source to placement (should decrease)
- Client satisfaction scores (should increase)

**Show Clients**: "Since using AI screening, our candidates have an 80% client interview success rate vs. industry average of 35%"

## Common Questions from Agencies

### Q: Won't this slow down my process?

**A**: No - it's on-demand! Candidates interview when convenient, usually within 24 hours. No scheduling = no delays. You actually move FASTER because you're not scheduling multiple phone screens.

### Q: What if candidates refuse to do an AI interview?

**A**: In our experience, <5% object. Modern candidates appreciate: (1) No scheduling, (2) Interview on their time, (3) Faster process. Frame it as "We use AI for initial technical screening so we can fast-track great candidates."

### Q: Can I charge clients more if I use this?

**A**: Many agencies do! You're providing more thorough vetting and higher quality candidates. Some agencies charge a premium "technical vetting fee" or justify higher placement fees with better results.

### Q: How do I sell this to conservative clients?

**A**: Focus on results, not technology. *"We've added an additional screening layer that has increased our candidate success rate from 35% to 75%. Would you like to see a sample evaluation report?"*

### Q: What about candidates who are nervous with AI?

**A**: The AI interviewer is designed to be friendly and conversational. Candidates report it's LESS stressful than live interviews because: (1) No judgment if they take time to think, (2) Can pause if needed, (3) No interviewer bias.

### Q: Can I use this for non-technical roles?

**A**: Absolutely! Create rubrics for:
- Sales roles (objection handling, closing)
- Customer success (problem-solving scenarios)
- Project management (prioritization, communication)
- Healthcare (clinical scenarios)
- Any role where you need to assess specific competencies

### Q: What if my niche is very specialized?

**A**: Perfect use case! Create custom questions based on your niche. Examples:
- Medical device sales ‚Üí Regulatory knowledge
