# Create New Job Posting

Complete guide to creating job postings with full control over every detail and configuration option.

## Overview

The **Create New Job** feature provides comprehensive control over every aspect of your job posting and interview setup. Unlike Quick Create, which uses AI to automate the process, Create New Job gives you granular control over:

- Detailed job information and requirements
- Custom interview configurations
- Question and rubric assignments
- Email template selection
- Application workflow settings
- Advanced scheduling and deadline options

This feature is ideal for positions that require specialized interview processes, custom evaluation criteria, or unique application workflows.

## When to Use Create New Job vs. Quick Create

### Use Create New Job When:
- You need complete control over interview settings
- The position requires custom evaluation rubrics
- You want to attach specific interview questions from the start
- You need to configure advanced application workflows
- The role has unique requirements not suited for default settings
- You're setting up a complex multi-stage interview process

### Use Quick Create When:
- You have a job description ready to paste
- You need to create a posting quickly
- Standard interview settings work for your needs
- You can refine details after initial creation

## Prerequisites

Before creating a new job posting, ensure you have:
- An active AceUnlock account with recruiter or administrator privileges
- Company profile configured in your organization settings
- (Optional) Custom interview questions prepared
- (Optional) Evaluation rubrics created
- (Optional) Email templates ready for candidate communication

## Getting Started

### Step 1: Access Create New Job

1. Navigate to the **Job Postings** dashboard
2. Click the **Create New Job** button (blue button in the top-right corner)
3. A comprehensive job creation form will open

## Job Posting Form Sections

### Section 1: Basic Job Details

This section captures the fundamental information about the position.

**Required Fields:**

- **Job Title**: The official title of the position
  - Examples: "Senior React Developer," "Marketing Manager," "Data Scientist"
  - Be specific and clear to attract the right candidates

- **Employment Type**: Select from dropdown
  - Full-Time
  - Part-Time
  - Contract
  - Internship

- **Location**: Where the job is based
  - Enter city and state/province
  - Select "Remote" if applicable
  - Can specify "Hybrid" with location details

- **Department**: The team or department this role belongs to
  - Select from your organization's departments
  - Helps with internal organization and reporting

**Optional Fields:**

- **Salary Range**: Compensation information
  - Can specify as range or "Competitive"
  - Displayed to candidates if included

- **Reports To**: The position this role reports to
  - Helps candidates understand organizational structure

### Section 2: Job Description and Requirements

Craft a compelling and comprehensive job description.

**Job Description:**
- Use the rich text editor to format your description
- Include an engaging overview of the role
- Detail key responsibilities and day-to-day activities
- Describe the team and company culture
- Highlight growth opportunities

**Requirements:**
- List required qualifications
- Specify years of experience needed
- Detail technical skills and tools
- Include soft skills and competencies
- Note any certifications or education requirements

**Preferred Qualifications (Optional):**
- List nice-to-have skills
- Specify bonus qualifications
- Mention additional experience that's valued but not required

**Best Practice:** Create detailed, realistic descriptions. The AI interviewer will use this information to generate relevant interview questions.

<!-- Image placeholder: Screenshot of Job Description section with rich text editor -->

### Section 3: Interview Settings and Configuration

Configure how the AI interview will be conducted for this position.

**Interview Duration:**
- Set the expected interview length (e.g., 15, 30, 45, or 60 minutes)
- Default: 30 minutes
- Adjusts the number of questions asked

**Interview Mode:**
- **Standard**: Traditional Q&A format
- **Conversational**: More natural, adaptive dialogue
- **Technical**: Focus on technical problem-solving (for tech roles)

**Interview Focus Areas:**
- Select key areas to emphasize during interviews
- Options include: Technical Skills, Problem-Solving, Leadership, Communication, Cultural Fit
- AI interviewer will weight these areas accordingly

**Recording Options:**
- Enable/disable audio recording of interviews
- Enable/disable video recording (if applicable)
- Configure transcript generation

**Retake Policy:**
- Allow/disallow candidates to retake interviews
- Set number of allowed attempts if retakes are enabled
- Specify cooling-off period between attempts

### Section 4: Attach Questions and Rubrics

Link custom interview questions and evaluation rubrics to your job posting.

**Interview Questions:**

1. Click **Add Questions** button
2. Browse your question bank or search by keyword
3. Select questions to attach to this posting
4. Mark questions as:
   - **Mandatory**: Must be asked to every candidate
   - **Optional**: AI may ask based on conversation flow
5. Reorder questions by priority

**Why attach questions?**
- Ensure consistency across all candidate interviews
- Ask must-have screening questions
- Cover specific topics important to your organization

**Evaluation Rubrics:**

1. Click **Attach Rubric** button
2. Select from your organization's custom rubrics
3. Preview rubric criteria
4. Attach multiple rubrics if needed (e.g., technical + cultural fit)

**Why use rubrics?**
- Standardize candidate evaluation
- Align assessment with organizational values
- Enable objective comparison across candidates
- Ensure consistency in scoring

### Section 5: Email Template Configuration

Select email templates for automated candidate communication.

**Available Email Types:**

- **Application Confirmation**: Sent when candidate submits application
- **Interview Invitation**: Sent when inviting candidates to interview
- **Interview Reminder**: Sent before scheduled interview time
- **Interview Completion**: Sent after candidate completes interview
- **Rejection Email**: Sent to candidates not moving forward
- **Offer Email**: Sent to selected candidates

**For each email type:**
1. Select from your organization's email templates
2. Preview the template content
3. Customize merge fields (e.g., candidate name, job title)
4. Set sending triggers and timing

**If no template is selected:**
- System default emails will be used
- You can still customize emails manually when sending

**Related Documentation:**
- [Email Templates Guide](./email-template.md)

<!-- Image placeholder: Screenshot of Email Template selection interface -->

### Section 6: Application Deadline and Settings

Configure application timeline and additional settings.

**Application Deadline:**
- Set the last date/time for candidates to apply
- Can set as specific date or "Ongoing"
- Posting automatically moves to "Closed" after deadline

**Application Questions:**
- Add custom pre-screening questions
- Specify required fields beyond standard application
- Examples: "Are you authorized to work in [Country]?", "What is your expected salary?"

**Visibility Settings:**
- **Public**: Anyone with the link can apply
- **Private**: Only invited candidates can access
- **Internal**: Only visible to your organization members

**Notification Settings:**
- Configure who receives notifications when candidates apply
- Set notification frequency (immediate, daily digest, weekly)
- Customize notification content

**Advanced Settings:**
- Enable/disable anonymous applications
- Set application limits (max number of applications)
- Configure automatic screening criteria

## Finalizing Your Job Posting

### Review Your Configuration

Before creating the posting:

1. **Review all sections** for accuracy and completeness
2. **Verify interview settings** match your hiring process
3. **Check attached questions and rubrics** are appropriate
4. **Preview email templates** for professionalism
5. **Confirm deadline** is realistic for your hiring timeline

### Save Options

**Save as Draft:**
- Click **Save as Draft** to save without publishing
- Job posting is not live and candidates cannot access it
- You can edit and refine before publishing
- Visible only to your organization's recruiters and admins

**Publish Immediately:**
- Click **Create and Publish** to make the posting live
- Job posting link is immediately active
- Email notifications are sent based on your settings
- Candidates can start applying right away

**Save as Template:**
- Option to save this configuration as a template
- Useful for recurring similar positions
- Speeds up future job posting creation

## After Creation

Once your job posting is created:

1. **Job Posting Link**: A unique URL is generated for candidate applications
2. **Dashboard Display**: The posting appears in your Job Postings dashboard
3. **Share**: Copy the link to share via email, social media, or job boards
4. **Monitor**: Track applications and interview completions in real-time
5. **Edit Anytime**: Modify posting details as needed (except if candidates have already applied to certain fields)

## Best Practices

### Crafting Effective Job Postings

1. **Be Transparent**: Clearly state responsibilities, requirements, and expectations
2. **Use Keywords**: Include industry-standard terms for better discoverability
3. **Show Personality**: Let your company culture shine through
4. **Be Inclusive**: Use inclusive language and highlight diversity commitments
5. **Proofread**: Check for typos and grammatical errors

### Interview Configuration

1. **Match Complexity to Role**: Don't over-complicate interviews for entry-level positions
2. **Test Your Settings**: Create a test posting to experience the candidate flow
3. **Balance AI and Custom**: Use AI flexibility with strategic custom questions
4. **Set Realistic Durations**: Consider candidate experience when setting interview length

### Questions and Rubrics

1. **Attach Strategically**: Don't overload with too many mandatory questions
2. **Align with Role**: Ensure questions and rubrics match the position level
3. **Update Regularly**: Review and refine questions based on hiring outcomes
4. **Stay Legal**: Avoid questions that could be discriminatory

### Email Communication

1. **Professional Tone**: Ensure templates reflect your brand voice
2. **Mobile-Friendly**: Templates should render well on mobile devices
3. **Clear CTAs**: Include clear next steps and action items
4. **Timely Sending**: Configure appropriate timing for automated emails

## Tips for Success

### For Technical Roles
- Use the Technical interview mode
- Attach coding or problem-solving questions
- Set longer interview duration (45-60 minutes)
- Include technical skills in focus areas

### For Leadership Roles
- Emphasize Leadership and Problem-Solving in focus areas
- Attach behavioral interview questions
- Use comprehensive evaluation rubrics
- Set longer interview duration for depth

### For Entry-Level Roles
- Keep interview duration moderate (20-30 minutes)
- Focus on cultural fit and learning potential
- Use standard interview mode
- Include growth opportunities in description

### For High-Volume Hiring
- Set up robust pre-screening questions
- Use automatic screening criteria
- Enable efficient email automation
- Consider application limits if needed

## Troubleshooting

### Common Issues

**Problem**: Can't find certain departments or locations
- **Solution**: Update your company profile settings to add missing options

**Problem**: Custom questions aren't appearing in list
- **Solution**: Ensure questions are marked as "Active" in Question Management

**Problem**: Email templates are missing
- **Solution**: Create email templates first in Email Templates section

**Problem**: Can't publish posting
- **Solution**: Check that all required fields are completed (marked with asterisk)
