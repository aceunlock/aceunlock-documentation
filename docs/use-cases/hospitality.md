## Best Practices for Hospitality Hiring

### 1. Always Use Video for Guest-Facing Roles

**Why**: You need to see how candidates present themselves. Phone screens miss:
- Professional appearance
- Smile and warmth
- Non-verbal communication
- Camera presence (translates to guest presence)

**Rule**: No in-person interviews without video interview first.

### 2. Create Role-Specific Scenario Questions

**Bad Question**: "How would you handle a difficult customer?"
**Good Question**: "A guest approaches the front desk at 2 AM complaining loudly about noise from the adjacent room. Other guests are nearby. What do you do?"

**Why Specific Scenarios Work**: Shows real problem-solving, not rehearsed answers.

### 3. Screen for Flexibility Early

**Add Questions**:
- "What days and times are you available?"
- "Can you work holidays? Which ones are you unavailable?"
- "How do you feel about split shifts?"
- "What's your ideal schedule?"

**Weight Heavily**: If flexibility is critical, make it 20-25% of your rubric.

### 4. Use Rubrics Consistently

**Don't**: Let each manager use their own criteria
**Do**: Create standard rubric, all managers use it

**Result**: Fair hiring, consistent quality, easier to defend decisions.

### 5. Review Videos, Not Just Scores

**The Score**: Tells you who passed your bar
**The Video**: Shows you WHO they are

**Watch For**:
- Genuine smile and warmth (can't fake this)
- Energy level (do they seem tired/disinterested?)
- Communication style (formal vs. casual vs. friendly)
- Setting (professional background? quiet space?)

### 6. Move Fast on Great Candidates

**Reality**: Great hospitality candidates get multiple offers

**When you see a star**:
- Call/text same day
- Offer in-person interview within 24 hours
- Make offer within 48 hours if possible

**AceUnlock helps**: You can review interviews 24/7, reach out immediately.

### 7. Track Quality Metrics

**Monitor**:
- 90-day retention rate (by hiring manager)
- Guest satisfaction scores (new hires vs. tenured)
- No-show rate to in-person interviews
- Time-to-fill per role

**Improve**: Use data to refine rubrics and questions.
