### 3. Prepare Your Stories

Use the **STAR method** to prepare 5-7 stories:

**STAR Format**:
- **S**ituation - Set the context
- **T**ask - What needed to be done
- **A**ction - What YOU did specifically
- **R**esult - The outcome (quantify if possible)

**Example categories**:
- Achievement/Success story
- Challenge/Problem-solving
- Teamwork/Collaboration
- Leadership
- Conflict resolution
- Learning/Growth
- Failure and what you learned

### 4. Practice Common Questions

Prepare for these common questions:

**About You**:
- "Tell me about yourself"
- "Why are you interested in this role?"
- "What are your strengths and weaknesses?"
- "Where do you see yourself in 5 years?"

**Experience**:
- "Tell me about a time when..."
- "Describe a situation where you..."
- "Give an example of..."

**Behavioral**:
- "How do you handle stress/pressure?"
- "Describe your work style"
- "How do you prioritize tasks?"

**Role-Specific**:
- Technical questions related to the job
- Industry knowledge
- Tools and methodologies

### 5. Prepare Your Environment

**Day before**:
- Choose your interview location (quiet, private)
- Test your internet speed (5+ Mbps recommended)
- Charge your device or plan to plug in
- Choose professional outfit
- Prepare resume and notes for reference

**1 hour before**:
- Set up your space
- Adjust lighting (face a light source)
- Clean/organize background
- Silence phone, close other apps
- Notify household members you're interviewing

**15 minutes before**:
- Open the interview link
- Test camera and microphone
- Do final appearance check
- Take deep breaths and relax

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### âŒ Technical Mistakes

**Poor setup**:
- Bad lighting (too dark, backlit)
- Unprofessional background
- Camera positioned too low/high
- Poor audio quality

**Environment issues**:
- Noisy location (traffic, TV, roommates)
- Interruptions (pets, children, deliveries)
- Unstable internet connection
- Phone notifications

**Solution**: Test everything 30 minutes before, choose quiet private space

### âŒ Content Mistakes

**Vague or generic answers**:
- "I'm a hard worker and team player"
- "I'm passionate about this field"
- No specific examples or metrics

**Solution**: Use STAR method with specific, quantified examples

**Talking too much or too little**:
- 10-minute rambling answers
- One-sentence responses

**Solution**: Aim for 2-3 minutes per answer, detailed but concise

**Negativity**:
- Complaining about past employers
- Blaming others for failures
- Negative attitude

**Solution**: Frame challenges positively, focus on what you learned

**Lack of preparation**:
- Don't know anything about the company
- Can't explain why you want the role
- No questions to ask

**Solution**: Research and prepare beforehand

### âŒ Presentation Mistakes

**Reading from script**:
- Obvious you're reading
- Eyes looking away from camera
- Monotone delivery

**Solution**: Prepare bullet points, speak naturally

**Low energy**:
- Monotone voice
- No enthusiasm
- Slouching

**Solution**: Show genuine interest, sit up, vary your tone

**Being late/rushing**:
- Starting interview with stress
- Not testing equipment first
- Forgetting interview deadline

**Solution**: Complete 1-2 days before deadline, give yourself plenty of time

## Special Situations

### For Entry-Level Candidates

**If you lack work experience**:
- Use examples from school projects, internships, volunteer work
- Highlight transferable skills
- Show enthusiasm and willingness to learn
- Focus on potential, not just past experience

**Key strengths**:
- Fresh perspective
- Eagerness to learn
- Adaptability
- Up-to-date education

### For Career Changers

**Address the transition**:
- Explain why you're changing careers
- Highlight transferable skills
- Show relevant education/training
- Demonstrate genuine interest

**Connect the dots**:
- Relate past experience to new role
- Show how your unique background adds value
- Emphasize adaptability

### For Technical Roles

**Prepare for technical questions**:
- Review key concepts and technologies
- Prepare to explain your technical projects
- Be ready to discuss problem-solving approaches
- Practice explaining technical topics simply

**Show depth**:
- Discuss trade-offs and alternatives
- Explain your decision-making process
- Demonstrate continuous learning

### For Leadership Roles

**Focus on**:
- Leadership philosophy and style
- Examples of leading teams
- Change management experience
- Strategic thinking
- Results and business impact

**Quantify**:
- Team size, budget managed
- Revenue impact, cost savings
- Project scope and complexity
